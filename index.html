Life was a direct communion of man with heaven and the growth of the corn the only time scale. Yet one year the rain left and the seed was lost in the barren field. Huarcha, the medicine man, invoked guardian spirits and searched for the evil spell (curse) in the guts of the cuyes (guinea pigs); when he raised his head his look was ferocious:

"A virgin must die in the volcano to calm the gods. The maiden that turns fifteen years old in the next moon will be scarified", he condemned. When Nina Paccha passed, the feeling of the inevitable extinguished the words. Nina was beautiful. She had soft lips, dark eyes as a sparrow's wings and braided hair falling on her back. Her name meant Fountain of Light.

Let's run away Nina!, he begged as the night wrapped the young woman in soft shroud. Taita Imbabura (father Imbabura) will chase and punish us! No!, Huarcha lies, Taita is kind and will protect our love! When Huarcha saw that Nina had escaped he started his search. We will catch them! The gods will not be hoaxed!, he screamed.

Guatalqu surprised turned his head and when he saw Nina, the heaven illuminated splitting the dawn like an axe swing. The girl disappeared as a clean water fountain started to extend itself over the plain. Guatalqu still holding on to the lace of hair fell to the ground imploring: Taita (father) punish me too!. Nina became a lake because I stole her!. And then a lightning crossed over Rey Loma (king mountain) and a leafy tree appeared, it was Guatalqu.
